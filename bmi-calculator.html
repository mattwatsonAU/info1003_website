BMI

What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is used by physicians around the world to determine whether an individual is underweight, overweight or an ideal weight according to their height and age. It is useful for assessing the health risks involved with Cardiovascular Disease which rises significantly for overweight individuals.

How is it calculated?

It is calculated by dividing your weight in kilograms by your height in metres squared.

Interpreting the BMI

< 18.5: Underweight and possibly malnourished, 18.5-24.9: Healthy weight range for young and middle-aged adults, 25.0-29.9: Overweight, >30: Obese

Exceptions to take note of

Children: It is much harder to interpret the BMI of children (those under 18) as they are constantly growing and at different rates. Thus the standard BMI calculator may not be as useful for assessing the health of children compared to adults.

Body Builders: Body builders and those who have a lot of muscle may also not be assessed as well due to the fact BMI does not differentiate between fat and muscle

Height – Since BMI includes height, obesity amongst shorter people may be overestimated and amongst younger people may be underestimated. Therefore, BMI should not be used as a guide for adults who are very short (less than 150 cm) or very tall (more than 190 cm).

People of different ethnic groups – People of various ethnic groups vary in their average heights, weights and levels of body/fat or muscle. Asians often have more body fat at any given height compared to those from European descent. Thus the risks posed are often different for these ethnic groups and should also be noted. x 703
